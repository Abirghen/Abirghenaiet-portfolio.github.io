COPY vs. CONTENT

These 2 terms are used interchangeably at times. It doesn't help that many writers are skilled at writing both copy and content. They are able to adapt for each project's need and audience. However, for the sake of clarity, there are distinctions. Copy is the messaging that drives your audience to a desired action. Sometimes that action is a purchase, sometimes it is a task.

  • WHAT MAKES A PROJECT RESULT IN A GREAT FINISHED PRODUCT?

    Provide *thorough* information about the content you need. Examples from the competition are helpful for style, but YOUR business should be unique and have a unique selling proposition (USP). You can't say "we do everything that XYZ company does; reword their website for us" and expect that to suffice. Information specific to your offering, your experience, and your target audience should be provided.

  • QUALITY OR COUNT?

    Understand where high word counts are appropriate. LONG product/service descriptions don't help. They overwhelm and confuse. Use blog posts if you want to focus on word counts for the purposes of page ranking. Even then, the content has to be good quality and not rambling redundancies.

  • KNOW YOUR LANGUAGES

    Many clients have language chanllenges which affect their overall approval. Keep in mind that language barriers can get in the way if you're trying to approve content/copy that is a language in which *you aren't fluent*.
